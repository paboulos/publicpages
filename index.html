MIDI and Microcontrollers

MIDI is a ubiquitous protocol that allows different musical instruments and devices to communicate with each other using digital messages. MIDI was first published in 1983, the outcome of collaboration between various manufacturers. MIDI 2.0 is an expansion of the original protocol. It builds on the core principles, architecture, and semantics. Microcontrollers are frequently used as hardware to generate and transmit MIDI information in real-time.
